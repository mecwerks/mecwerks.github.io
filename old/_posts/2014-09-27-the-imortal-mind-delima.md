#The "Immortal Mind" Delima

As most of you know, the idea of transferring your conscience to a computer and living forever inside a machine has been around for years. Many movies and shows theorize what would happen if this were actually accomplished but no one knows for sure what the outcome would be.

Imagine this, all of your memories, tendencies, and thoughts dumped into an artificial intelligence. Would this machine be you? Would you be the machine? 

My answer: No.

In my opinion, a conscience is the product of your thoughts, emotions, experiences, and memories as they are this exact moment. Even if all of those things were copied exactly to an AI, you cannot feel things the AI does and the AI cannot feel anything you do (at least not in the same way). There is nothing connecting the two of you.

Theoretically, if you died the exact instance that your mind finished copying to the AI, the AI would feel as if it was you, it would react similar to how you would. It may even know how it felt to die moments before. But the way you think now would be gone forever. The AI would start thinking based on the information it has (which now that your dead you cannot experience it). The AI technically would be an exact copy of you but at the moment it started processing your information it started experiencing everything differently from how you would see it.

Think of it like two people on a date. Both people are in the same place, at the same time, seeing and hearing the same things. After the night is over neither person is any closer to being the other just because they shared the same memory. As soon as they departed from each other (and even during the date) they don't share the same thought processes and they don't feel what the other does (other than if by chance or being expressed directly). They are not the same mind.

I think I explained it as correctly as I could. If there are any questions please feel free to ask.
